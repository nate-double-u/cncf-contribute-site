## The .project Repository Rollout Has Started

The CNCF is officially rolling out .project repositories across all hosted projects. This  initiative is designed to streamline governance, centralize metadata tracking, and make resource access much smoother for everyone.(Blog: Introducing .project: Standardized Project Metadata, Rolled Out for You)

If your project is currently applying to move levels within the CNCF, this rollout is being prioritized for your organization right now. However, all CNCF projects need to start prepping their data.

To ensure a seamless transition and prevent any interruptions to your access to CNCF resources, the Service Desk, and maintainer mailing lists, etc., we need all hands on deck to get maintainer records updated.

### Your Action Items

Please share these steps with your project's maintainer team and complete them as soon as possible:

* **Keep Maintainer Data Up to Date:** Ensure your project's existing maintainer or CODEOWNERS files in your main repository are accurate. The CNCF team will be automatically provisioning your new .project repository and generating an initial Pull Request (PR) based on these files.
* **Link GitHub Handles to LFID:** Every listed maintainer must link their GitHub handle to their Linux Foundation ID (LFID). You can do this quickly at OpenProfile.
* **Verify Email Settings:** Head over to OpenProfile Email Management and ensure your primary email preferences are correctly configured. These addresses are used directly for access management.
* **Review and Merge Your PR:** Once the CNCF team provisions your .project repository, look out for the pending maintainer verification PR. Review, update, and merge it promptly!

### Questions or Support?

If your team runs into any issues or has questions about the transition, please reach out directly to the CNCF project team or open a ticket via the CNCF Service Desk.

Thank you for helping us make CNCF governance cleaner and more efficient!
